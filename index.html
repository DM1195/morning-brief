WOMEN FOUNDER GRANTS FUNDING

Four New Funds Women's Health Founders Should Know About

FEMTECH INSIDER · May 11

Four investment funds spanning the US and UK have opened or closed in recent weeks, each targeting women's health and female-founded companies. TechThrive Ventures launched a $7M pre-seed fund while Wisdom Ventures closed an oversubscribed $77.7M Fund II — backed by Reid Hoffman and Evan Sharp — writing $1–5M checks across roughly 40 AI-enabled health and wellness startups. In the UK, the Araya Sie Fund raised £7.5M to back female founders at pre-seed and seed, and the British Business Bank committed £1M alongside Angel Academe for seed-to-Series-A companies requiring at least one female founder. For founders operating anywhere in the femtech or women's health space, this is the densest cluster of targeted capital to appear in months — and the Wisdom Ventures LP roster signals institutional legitimacy, not just a wave of marketing spend.

Plate I  ·  Oil on canvas
Improvisation No. 30 (Cannons)
Vasily Kandinsky  ·  1913

Kandinsky named this 'Improvisation' — a term borrowed from music — because he believed painting could bypass language and touch the soul directly, the way a chord does. The cannons, which he reportedly didn't notice until the painting was done, arrived unbidden from the war talk saturating Berlin in 1913. What the work actually shows is not warfare but the feeling of living inside a culture on the verge of rupture.

Plate II  ·  Bronze
Woman (Elevation)
Gaston Lachaise  ·  Modeled 1912–15, cast 1927

Lachaise spent three years circling this figure before the first cast was made. The woman is Isabel Dutaud Nagle — his partner, his subject, his reason for crossing an ocean — and the slightly impossible proportions (that narrow waist, the deliberate imbalance of scale) are not distortion but intensity. Plate III  ·  Oil on canvas
Lozenge Composition with Yellow, Black, Blue, Red, and Gray
Piet Mondrian  ·  1921

The canvas is a square hung on its corner — a decision that sounds like a provocation but is actually an inquiry. Mondrian spent his career asking what it means for a line to be contained within a frame versus to imply something beyond it, and in this lozenge format the single line extending to the slanted edge does exactly that: it escapes. He hung these paintings high on the wall, so the diagonal edges would invoke the full white expanse of the room around them.

Plate IV  ·  Oil on canvas
The Fountain, Villa Torlonia, Frascati, Italy
John Singer Sargent  ·  1907

1907 was the year Sargent walked away from portrait commissions, a form that had made him famous and that he'd come to find suffocating. He spent the summer with two painter friends — Wilfrid and Jane de Glehn — at an Italian villa, and this is the record of that pause: two people posed in dappled light across several rain-soaked, interrupted sessions. The 'spontaneity' you see here took weeks, which is the private joke of Impressionism — the illusion of effortlessness as a hard-won achievement.
